\(\vec{r}_0 = \vec{r}_1 + \lambda_0 \cdot \vec{E}\) (da \(P_0\) auf \(q\) liegt, muss es ein \(\lambda_0\) geben, was Gleichung erfüllt) 

<|ref|>text<|/ref|><|det|>[[102, 515, 625, 552]]<|/det|>
Einheitsnormalvelkor aus \(q\) : \(\hat{n} = \frac{1}{|\vec{r}_0|} \cdot \vec{r}_0\) 

<|ref|>equation<|/ref|><|det|>[[108, 572, 822, 644]]<|/det|>
\[ \begin{aligned} \rightarrow <\hat{n}, \vec{r}_0> &= <\hat{n}, \vec{r}_1 + \lambda_0 \cdot \vec{E}> = <\hat{n}, \vec{r}_1> + <\hat{n}, \lambda_0 \cdot \vec{E}> \\ &= <\hat{n}, \vec{r}_1> \end{aligned} \]

<|ref|>equation<|/ref|><|det|>[[162, 670, 690, 741]]<|/det|>
\[ \begin{aligned} <\hat{n}, \vec{r}_0> &= <\frac{1}{|\vec{r}_0|} \cdot \vec{r}_0, \vec{r}_0> = \frac{1}{|\vec{r}_0|} <\vec{r}_0, \vec{r}_0> \\ &= |\vec{r}_0| \end{aligned} \]

<|ref|>equation<|/ref|><|det|>[[108, 750, 580, 780]]<|/det|>
\[ \rightarrow <\hat{n}, \vec{r}_0> = <\hat{n}, \vec{r}_1> = |\vec{r}_0| = d \]
