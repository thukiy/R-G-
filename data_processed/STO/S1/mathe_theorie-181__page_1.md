# Mittelwert und Varians einer linearen Funktion 

<|ref|>equation<|/ref|><|det|>[[60, 50, 444, 80]]<|/det|>
\[ \bar{z} = aX + b, \quad a, b \in \mathbb{R} \]

<|ref|>text<|/ref|><|det|>[[55, 88, 400, 118]]<|/det|>
1) Mittelwert \(E(\bar{z}) = \mu_z\) 

<|ref|>equation<|/ref|><|det|>[[312, 128, 660, 197]]<|/det|>
\[ \begin{aligned} = E(aX+b) &= a \cdot E(X) + b \\ &= a \cdot \mu_X + b \end{aligned} \]

<|ref|>text<|/ref|><|det|>[[55, 205, 260, 237]]<|/det|>
2) Varianz \(\sigma_z^2\) 

<|ref|>equation<|/ref|><|det|>[[92, 245, 592, 275]]<|/det|>
\[ \text{Var}(\bar{z}) = \text{Var}(a \cdot X + b) = a^2 \cdot \text{Var}(X) \]

<|ref|>equation<|/ref|><|det|>[[92, 283, 280, 316]]<|/det|>
\[ \sigma_z^2 = a^2 \cdot \sigma_X^2 \]

<|ref|>text<|/ref|><|det|>[[55, 324, 365, 355]]<|/det|>
3) Standardabweichung 

<|ref|>equation<|/ref|><|det|>[[92, 363, 285, 393]]<|/det|>
\[ \sigma_z = |a| \cdot \sigma_X \]

<|ref|>text<|/ref|><|det|>[[20, 421, 797, 450]]<|/det|>
**Bsp**: \(X\) sei außervariabe mit \(E(X) = \mu\) und \(\text{Var} = \sigma^2\). 

<|ref|>text<|/ref|><|det|>[[123, 460, 453, 485]]<|/det|>
diverse Transformation: 

<|ref|>equation<|/ref|><|det|>[[140, 492, 480, 604]]<|/det|>
\[ \begin{aligned} \bar{z} &= \frac{x-\mu}{\sigma} = \frac{1}{\sigma} \cdot X - \frac{\mu}{\sigma} \\ \mu_z &= \frac{1}{\sigma} \cdot \mu_X - \frac{\mu_X}{\sigma} = 0 \\ \sigma_z^2 &= \left( \frac{1}{\sigma_X} \right)^2 \cdot \sigma_X^2 = 1 \end{aligned} \]

<|ref|>text<|/ref|><|det|>[[125, 635, 755, 661]]<|/det|>
→ Transformation nennt sich Standardisierung, 

<|ref|>text<|/ref|><|det|>[[184, 672, 720, 700]]<|/det|>
\(Z\) heisst standardisierte außervariabe
